Covid cluster

As the coronavirus pandemic picks up speed around the world, scientists are increasingly interested in the phenomenon of disease clusters. They happen everywhere: At bars and barbecues, gyms and factories, schools, ships and churches. But why do these clusters occur—and why are they so important?

One consequence of COVID-19 clustering is that if you find a single case, the likelihood is high that he will not infect anyone else. But there is also a good chance that that person was infected at a superspreading event.

Doing this also gives health officials more information about where such events happen.
